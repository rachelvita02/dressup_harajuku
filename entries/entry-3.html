what's gyaru?

Gyaru fashion is a vibrant and bold style that originated in Japan during the 1990s. It emphasizes a rebellious attitude, often challenging traditional beauty standards through dramatic makeup, tanned skin, and daring clothing choices. The fashion features distinct substyles, including "Hime gyaru" (princess gyaru) known for its elegant, doll-like aesthetic and "Kuro gyaru," which highlights darker tans and edgy looks. Gyaru makeup is iconic, with heavy eyeliner, voluminous lashes, and sometimes colored contact lenses to achieve larger-looking eyes. Hairstyles typically involve big, voluminous curls or straight, teased hair. Accessories, such as oversized nails and extravagant jewelry, play a key role in completing the look. Over time, the style has evolved, though its spirit of individuality and boldness remains intact. Today, gyaru fashion continues to inspire street style globally while maintaining its roots in Japanese youth culture.

rachel's rating

Gyaru fashion is one of my favorite styles personally because of how 2000s the clothing is, and Hime gyaru is my favorite gyaru subculture because of the pink color palette and floral and bow motifs. I love how unapologetically feminine and rebellious this style is, and it makes me wish I was a teenager in Japan during its peak.The eye makeup is definitely one of my favorite aspects of the style, and I like how it transforms the eye in a doll-like way. I’ve tried out gyaru makeup a few times for fun and it’s very time consuming but it turns out so cool! I love shopping for second-hand Japanese clothing and I’m slowly collecting pieces from Liz Lisa and Jesus Diamante. Hopefully I’ll be able to incorporate the gyaru style into my everyday outfits!
