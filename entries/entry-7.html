mori kei

what's mori kei?

Mori girl is a J-Fashion style created in 2007. The style was formed as a counter style to the many hyper-girly fashion styles that were popular at the time. This style is considered a natural fashion, being centered around natural themes and a love of the forest. Mori has a cheerful, calm and simple aesthetic. Mori girl, self-described as 'forest girls', is a style reminiscent of a forest lifestyle - loose, light and airy clothing, layered with warm, bulky sweaters and scarves and tough boots for navigating the wilderness. Natural materials like cotton, linen, leather and wool reign supreme, although other materials are also sometimes utilized. The style contains mostly neutral shades, such as off-white, beiges and browns. Hairstyles in mori girl are typically long, loose and flowing, but can also be shorter and vary widely. A classic mori look includes very little makeup: a little blush on the cheeks, maybe a light gloss on the lips, and foundation are staples.The most common accessory in mori girl would by far be scarves, flowers in your hair, golden, rustic jewelry with nature themes, fingerless gloves, straw baskets, straw hats, and anything with lace.

rachel's rating

While I’m not a huge fan of earth tones, I love how mori kei fashion is very delicate and loose. Seeing how people layered clothes and accessories was really interesting and inspiring for me. I also like how this style emphasizes thrifting and repurposing clothes, and I think it makes it more accessible and sustainable for people to participate in the style. The use of lace, floral patterns, and knit materials caught my attention and are elements I’m naturally drawn to in the clothes I buy. Compared to some of the other styles, I feel like mori kei fashion is more practical to wear everyday because of how simple it can be.
